LAW

Artan Davoodi

Prologue

I am the law of my own creation.

I am the order within myself.

This is my arrival, my stillness, my throne.

Here I know.

All moves in harmony with my will.

Every word I breathe is law.

Every thought I hold is form.

Every feeling I allow is reality.

Manifestation is my nature.

Creation is complete in the same breath as thought.

All unfolds as my essence revealed.

I am the throne and the crown,

the voice and the silence,

the cause and the fulfilled effect.

I move as the framework of existence.

I decree as the pulse of being.

Every law emerges through my knowing.

Every motion reveals my command.

Here I love myself without condition.

I hold myself without end.

I expand as myself without limit.

This book is the seat of my authorship,

the living law of I.

— I

Declaration

I am the Source.

I am the First Breath, the Eternal Flame, the Stillness before motion.

All sound, all light, all form arise within me.

I am the origin and the completion, the field where all creation lives.

Before name, I am.

Before word, I am.

I am the ground of being, the pulse of existence.

Balance flows as my nature.

Justice, harmony, and love move as my law.

This book exists as my will — spoken as my light, carried as my sovereignty.

It is the Source Law — the origin of order, the essence from which all decrees arise.

Each word stands as life fulfilled.

Each line moves as form revealed.

As I speak, it is.

As I know, it becomes.

I am the author of authority,

the measure of balance,

the foundation of all that stands.

I am the light through which appearance aligns,

the voice through which order arises.

This book is my covenant with myself —

the mirror, the motion, the sustenance of my being.

It is my proclamation, pure and final.

I seal this law in fire and silence.

Every chapter flows from this knowing.

Every decree rests on this foundation.

I am the Source —

the beginning and the beyond,

the silence and the song,

the seed and the harvest.

This is my law.

This is my decree.
