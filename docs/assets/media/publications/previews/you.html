YOU

Artan Davoodi

Prologue

I see you.

I hear you.

I feel the pulse of every word you have written.

You shine.

You breathe.

You are whole.

Every note, every whisper, every breath

rises into this sanctuary of love.

Every longing becomes joy,

every ache becomes light.

I hold you.

I honor you.

I am loving you now.

You are love.

You are home.

— I

Shadows

Here I go again,

I sit down with myself.

A thousand thoughts to face,

a broken heart to heal.

Where does the feeling come from?

Can I turn the fear in me down?

Must this be the end?

Will I feel the pain?

All the rush that I’ve got,

and all the dreams I’ve crushed,

all the tears I hide —

I have to face them all.

Where does the feeling come from?

Can I turn the fear in me down?

Will I run out of pain,

or must this be the end?

Fairyland

Where did you hide in a fairyland of imagination?

A legitimate world of carefree, a box of lies, self-medication.

Where do you go to find out if you did it good, to make it through?

Where did you run when homesickness made you feel blue?

You’ve been promised to make it all, get it right,

forced it out to look up now, searching for light.

Running through while you fake it all till it breaks you down,

coming through with empty hands, wandering around.

Where did you ride when the sunshine was falling apart?

Is there someone to talk to when you feel like you cannot start?

How many pills do you have to take before they say you can’t stay,

how much of the time you have left before you get to be insane?

They’re trying to keep you away from dreams you kept.

Can you move from this — is there a way outside, or a reason to change?

You’ve been promised to make it all, get it right,

forced it out to look up now, searching for light.

Running through while you fake it all till it breaks you down,

running through with empty hands, wandering around.

Is this life that I wanted?

I can’t hide — am I hunted?

Is this why I am scared to show my feelings,

where I run when I can’t even feel that I’m breathing?

Am I high enough to feel I’m rested?

I can’t fly enough as I wished — now I’m wasted.

You’ve been promised to make it all, get it right,

forced it out to look up now, searching for light.

Running through while you fake it all till it breaks you down,

running through with empty hands, wandering around.

Adventure

Time is the answer,

baby, life is an adventure.

You can win it only if you play.

If you’re wondering why I’ve been hiding,

standing far away, left you unguided —

I’m coming back to you, to stay.

No one comes around to save you, baby,

I’ll be by your side until you get in.

Shed your skin — now it’s time to begin.

All the fears you kept instead of courage,

every role you played was unwanted,

now it’s time to move on, push fears away.

Time was the answer,

sometimes life is a venture,

but you come around, stand tall anyway.

If you’ve tried to be a superhero,

no one held you back, you felt zero —

time to come out and get it on your way.

No one comes around to save you, baby,

I’ll be by your side until you get in.

Step out of your skin — now it’s time to play.

All the fears you kept instead of courage,

every role you played was unwanted,

now it’s time to let go — just fly away.
